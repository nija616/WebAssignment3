What are the symptoms of depression?

▼

A depressive episode is different from regular mood fluctuations. They last most of the day, nearly every day, for at least two weeks. Other symptoms are also present, which may include: poor concentration, feelings of excessive guilt or low self-worth, hopelessness about the future, thoughts about dying or suicide, disrupted sleep, changes in appetite or weight, feeling very tired or low in energy

What should I do if I'm having a panic attack?

▼

During a panic attack, focus on slow, deep breathing, remind yourself that it's temporary, and use grounding techniques like feeling an object to stay present. If panic attacks are frequent, consider seeking professional help.

Does exercise really improve mental health?

▼

Yes, regular physical activity can reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression by releasing endorphins and improving sleep. Even moderate exercise like walking can have positive mental health effects.
